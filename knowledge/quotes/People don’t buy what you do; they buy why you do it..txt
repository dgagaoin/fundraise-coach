## “People don’t buy what you do; they buy why you do it.”

**Attribution:** *Simon Sinek*
**Source:** *Start With Why* (2009)
**Context:** Leadership theory, organizational psychology, and purpose‑driven communication

---

## Meaning & Expansion

This quote captures a fundamental truth about human behavior: **people are motivated by meaning before mechanics**.

Simon Sinek’s insight reframes influence away from features, tactics, and persuasion techniques and toward purpose. Decisions—especially emotional ones—are driven by belief, identity, and values.

---

### Why Before What

“What you do” explains function.
“Why you do it” explains intention.

People may understand the former, but they *commit* to the latter.

Purpose creates:

* Trust
* Loyalty
* Emotional resonance

Without a clear “why,” communication becomes transactional and fragile.

---

### Biology of Decision‑Making

Sinek ties this idea to neuroscience:

* The “why” speaks to the limbic system (emotion, trust, belonging)
* The “what” speaks to the neocortex (logic, language, analysis)

People often rationalize decisions logically after making them emotionally.

---

### Leadership and Culture

Organizations that lead with “why”:

* Inspire discretionary effort
* Retain people longer
* Build cultures of belief rather than compliance

This applies equally to customers, donors, employees, and teams.

---

## Unified Message

**Meaning creates movement.**

People follow purpose, not instructions. When belief is clear, action follows naturally.

---

## Modern Interpretation

In today’s environment, this principle is essential for:

* Sales and fundraising
* Leadership and management
* Brand trust
* Mission‑driven organizations

Those who articulate a compelling “why” earn commitment rather than compliance.
