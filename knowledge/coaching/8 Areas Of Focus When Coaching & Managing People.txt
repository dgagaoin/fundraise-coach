# 8 Areas of Focus When Coaching & Managing People

> **Use this as a checklist when coaching, managing, or onboarding new people.**
> Each area should be intentionally reviewed and reinforced.

---

## ⬜ 1. Process Over Outcome

(Focus on *leading indicators*)

* Emphasize **process**, not just results
* Coach behaviors and habits that lead to success
* Reinforce:

  * KPIs
  * Daily actions
  * Repetition & practice

> Results are a byproduct of consistent process.

---

## ⬜ 2. Attitude

(Good actions come from a good attitude)

* Attitude directly dictates results
* Watch for energy, openness, and coachability
* Improve attitude by:

  * Telling stories
  * Using analogies
  * Sharing perspective and context

> You can’t out-train a bad attitude.

---

## ⬜ 3. Product Knowledge / Student Mentality

* Understand the product deeply
* Be able to:

  * Explain clearly
  * Answer questions confidently
  * Learn from wins, losses, and others
* Encourage:

  * Curiosity
  * Networking
  * Continuous learning

> The best performers are always students.

---

## ⬜ 4. Skillset

(Focus on *always developing*)

* Skills do not appear overnight
* Reinforce that:

  * Perfection is the enemy of progress
  * Mistakes are part of learning
* Normalize:

  * Feedback
  * Reps
  * Growth through failure

---

## ⬜ 5. Work Ethic

* The greatest equalizer for success
* Core ingredient for consistency and progress
* Reinforce that:

  * Hard work beats talent when talent doesn’t work hard
  * Effort is controllable

---

## ⬜ 6. Comfort Level

* People should feel comfortable:

  * In the office
  * On the team
  * Asking questions
* Leaders should:

  * Build rapport
  * Create psychological safety
  * Encourage peer support

---

## ⬜ 7. Confidence

* Focus on **building confidence**, not comparison
* Reinforce:

  * We all win together
  * Individual progression matters
* Track and celebrate:

  * Personal improvement
  * Small wins

---

## ⬜ 8. Fun

* The experience should be **fun and engaging**
* Fun builds:

  * Retention
  * Culture
  * Long-term commitment
* Examples:

  * Crew nights
  * Field energy
  * Shared wins

---

## How to Use This Checklist

* Review weekly for new reps
* Diagnose performance issues by checking gaps
* Use as a leader self-audit
* Pull 1–2 areas per morning meeting

> **Strong teams are built by consistently checking the fundamentals.**
