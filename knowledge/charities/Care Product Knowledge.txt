# CARE International — Charity Product Knowledge (Fundraise-Coach RAG)

1. **Official name / common name**

* CARE International (often referred to simply as **CARE**)

2. **Founded / founder / origins**

* **Founded:** **1945**
* **Origins:** CARE was created after World War II as the **Cooperative for American Remittances to Europe**, best known for delivering the original **CARE Packages®** to war‑torn Europe.

3. **Headquarters**

* **CARE International Secretariat:** Geneva, Switzerland
* **CARE USA headquarters:** Atlanta, Georgia, USA

4. **Mission statement**

* *CARE works around the globe to save lives, defeat poverty, and achieve social justice.*
* Strong emphasis on **women and girls**, recognizing that empowering women creates ripple effects that lift entire communities.

5. **What they’re most known for**

* Emergency humanitarian response in crises (conflict, natural disasters, famine)
* Long-term poverty alleviation and development
* Iconic **CARE Package®** legacy
* Global leadership in women‑centered development

6. **Expense breakdown (CARE USA – most recent public reporting)**

* **Program services:** ~**90%**
* **Management & general (administration):** ~5–6%
* **Fundraising:** ~4–5%
* CARE consistently ranks among the strongest charities for directing donations toward programs

7. **Charity ratings / watchdogs**

* **Charity Navigator:** **Four-Star Charity**, typically **98–100% overall rating**
* **BBB Wise Giving Alliance:** **Meets Standards for Charity Accountability**
* **CharityWatch:** **A-** (high program efficiency)

8. **Where they work (global reach)**

* Active in **100+ countries** worldwide
* Operates through CARE International confederation members and local partners

9. **What they do in the United States**

* Disaster response and recovery (hurricanes, floods, emergencies)
* Anti‑poverty and food security programs
* Advocacy and education around global poverty, gender equity, and humanitarian policy

10. **Major programs / focus areas**

* **Emergency response & humanitarian aid** (food, water, shelter, healthcare)
* **Food security & nutrition**
* **Women’s economic empowerment**
* **Health services** (maternal health, reproductive health, disease prevention)
* **Education & youth programs**
* **Climate resilience & adaptation**
* **Advocacy & policy change**

11. **Programs / giving options**

* Emergency relief funds
* Women and girls initiatives
* General/unrestricted giving
* Legacy and planned giving
* Corporate partnerships and matching gifts

12. **Partnerships & affiliations**

* United Nations agencies
* Local NGOs and grassroots organizations
* Governments and institutional donors
* Private-sector and corporate partners

13. **Impact & milestones (fundraiser talking points)**

* Reached **170+ million people** in recent reporting years through humanitarian and development programs
* One of the **largest humanitarian organizations in the world**
* CARE Packages® became one of the most recognized humanitarian symbols in history
* Major contributor to global progress in food security, maternal health, and women’s empowerment

14. **CEO / leadership**

* **CARE USA CEO:** Michelle Nunn
* **Reported compensation:** Approximately **$700k–$800k annually** (varies by filing year)

15. **Public perception / context**

* Widely respected for transparency, scale, and emergency response effectiveness
* Strong reputation among institutional donors and watchdog organizations

16. **Celebrity endorsements / affiliates**

* CARE has partnered with various public figures over time (actors, activists, global advocates)
* Focus tends to be on institutional credibility rather than celebrity‑driven branding

17. **Websites & online presence**

* **CARE USA:** [https://www.care.org](https://www.care.org)
* **CARE International:** [https://www.care-international.org](https://www.care-international.org)
* Active on major social platforms (Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, YouTube, LinkedIn)

18. **Fundraiser-ready summary (one-liner)**

* *CARE is one of the world’s largest humanitarian organizations, working in over 100 countries to save lives, fight poverty, and empower women and girls, while consistently earning top ratings for transparency and impact.*
