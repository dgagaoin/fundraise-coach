# The Nature Conservancy (TNC) — Charity Product Knowledge (Fundraise‑Coach RAG)

1. **Official name / common name**

* The Nature Conservancy (TNC)

2. **Founded / founder / origins**

* **Founded:** 1951 (incorporated October 22, 1951)
* **Origins:** Emerged from the Ecologists’ Union; early leaders included Richard H. Pough and Victor E. Shelford

3. **Headquarters**

* **Arlington, Virginia, USA**

4. **Mission statement**

* *To conserve the lands and waters on which all life depends.*

5. **What they’re most known for**

* One of the world’s largest and most influential environmental nonprofits
* Science‑based land and water conservation
* Large‑scale habitat protection, climate solutions, and biodiversity preservation

6. **Expense breakdown (most recent public reporting)**

* **Program services:** ~73%
* **Management & general (administration):** ~15%
* **Fundraising:** ~12%
* (Percentages vary slightly year‑to‑year but consistently show the majority going to programs)

7. **Charity ratings / watchdogs**

* **Charity Navigator:** Four‑Star charity (96–99% overall rating)
* **BBB Wise Giving Alliance:** Meets Standards for Charity Accountability
* **CharityWatch:** B+

8. **Where they work (global reach)**

* Active in **80+ countries and territories**
* Conservation projects in **all 50 U.S. states**

9. **What they do in the United States**

* Protect and restore forests, wetlands, rivers, grasslands, and coastal areas
* Operate local preserves and conservation projects
* Work with farmers, ranchers, Indigenous communities, and governments
* Advance climate resilience and sustainable land‑use solutions

10. **Major programs / focus areas**

* **Land conservation & stewardship** (land purchases, easements, long‑term protection)
* **Water & freshwater conservation** (rivers, wetlands, drinking water protection)
* **Oceans & coastal conservation** (marine habitats, fisheries, reefs)
* **Climate change solutions** (nature‑based climate mitigation and adaptation)
* **Science & innovation** (data‑driven conservation planning)
* **Policy & partnerships** (working with governments and businesses)

11. **Partnerships**

* Collaborates with governments, Indigenous groups, local communities, NGOs, scientists, and corporations
* Known for pragmatic, cross‑sector partnerships to scale conservation impact

12. **Programs / giving options**

* Unrestricted/general conservation support
* Land and habitat protection funds
* Climate and water initiatives
* Membership‑based giving
* Major gifts, legacy giving, and corporate partnerships

13. **Impact & milestones (fundraiser talking points)**

* Helped protect **150+ million acres of land** worldwide
* Conserved **thousands of miles of rivers** and vast marine areas
* Over **1 million members and supporters** globally
* Longstanding leader in science‑based conservation since 1951

14. **CEO / leadership**

* **CEO:** Jennifer Morris
* **Reported compensation (recent filings):** Approximately **$900k+ annually**

15. **Public perception / context**

* Widely respected for effectiveness and scale
* Occasionally criticized for corporate partnerships, but consistently highly rated by watchdogs

16. **Celebrity endorsements / affiliates**

* No single long‑term flagship celebrity ambassador
* Emphasis is on institutional, scientific, and governmental partnerships

17. **Websites & online presence**

* Main website: **[https://www.nature.org](https://www.nature.org)**
* State and country‑specific pages hosted under nature.org
* Active on major social platforms (Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, YouTube, LinkedIn)

18. **Fundraiser‑ready summary (one‑liner)**

* *The Nature Conservancy is a global environmental nonprofit working in 80+ countries to protect the lands and waters all life depends on, using science‑based solutions and partnerships, and is consistently rated as a top‑tier, highly effective charity.*
