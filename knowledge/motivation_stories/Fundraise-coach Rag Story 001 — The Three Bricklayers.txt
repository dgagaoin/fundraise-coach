## Title

**The Three Bricklayers**

---

## Core Story (Full Version – 8–10 minutes)

There’s an old story about a traveler who comes across three men working at a construction site.
All three are doing the exact same task—laying bricks, one after another, under the hot sun.

Curious, the traveler asks the first man,
“What are you doing?”

The man barely looks up and says,
“I’m laying bricks. It’s hard work. The pay is okay. That’s it.”

The traveler nods and moves on to the second man, asking the same question.
“What are you doing?”

The second man pauses, wipes sweat from his brow, and replies,
“I’m building a wall. It’s long, repetitive work, but it’s honest. This wall needs to be strong, and I’m doing my part.”

Then the traveler approaches the third man.
Same bricks. Same sun. Same task.

But when asked,
“What are you doing?”
The third man straightens up, smiles, and says:

“I’m building a cathedral.”

He explains that one day, people will walk inside this place to find peace, inspiration, and hope.
Families will gather. History will be made here.
Long after he’s gone, something meaningful will remain.

All three men were doing the *same work*.
But only one of them understood what the work *meant*.

And that difference—how they framed the work—changed everything about how they experienced it.

---

## Condensed Versions

### 2-Minute Version

Three people are laying bricks.
One says, “I’m just laying bricks.”
Another says, “I’m building a wall.”
The third says, “I’m building a cathedral.”

Same work.
Same effort.
Completely different meaning.

The difference isn’t the task—it’s the story they tell themselves about *why* the task matters.

### 5-Minute Version

Three workers do the same job under the same conditions.
One feels trapped.
One feels responsible.
One feels inspired.

The only difference is perspective.

When you know what you’re building—and why—it transforms effort into purpose.

## Audience Adaptations

### Sports Lens

Every athlete runs drills.
Every athlete trains when no one’s watching.

Some think, “I’m just doing reps.”
Others think, “I’m preparing for the season.”
The great ones think, “I’m building a championship version of myself.”

Same workouts.
Different identity.

### Video Game Lens

Some players grind without thinking.
Others know they’re leveling up.

Same enemies.
Same quests.
But one sees meaningless repetition—
the other sees skill points, stats, and progress toward the final boss.

### Sales / Field Work Lens

Some reps think, “I’m just knocking doors.”
Others think, “I’m hitting numbers.”
The best think, “I’m building confidence, discipline, income, and a future.”

Same pitch.
Same streets.
Different outcomes.

---

## Message of the Story (Core Lesson)

**The work doesn’t change—your meaning does.**
**And meaning determines motivation, resilience, and results.**

---

## Leader Notes (Optional)

**Use this story when:**

* Reps feel stuck in repetition
* Energy is low mid-week
* New reps don’t yet “see” the bigger picture

**Follow-up question to ask the team:**

> “What are *you* building right now—bricks, a wall, or a cathedral?”
